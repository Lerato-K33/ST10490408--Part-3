Brief History  
StrongHer Fitness Spot (SFS) is a women-only fitness center founded by Lerato Khanyile in South Africa with the vision of creating a safe and empowering environment where women can focus on their physical and mental well-being. The idea of this gym emerged from the sexual harassment Lerato faces at the gym and male superiority. Many women in South Africa often feel unsafe or uncomfortable in public spaces, including mixed-gender gyms, which can discourage them from participating in fitness activities.  
  
Recognizing this problem, Lerato established SFS as a space designed exclusively for women. The purpose of the organization is to provide a supportive and secure fitness environment where women can exercise confidently without fear of harassment, judgement, or intimidation. The gym focuses not only on physical training but also on empowering women through wellness programs, group fitness sessions, and motivational workshops that promote confidence, independence, and self-care.  
From its founding, SFS aimed to build a strong community among women by encouraging, mutual support and inclusivity to also trans women, the gym promotes the idea that fitness can be a powerful tool for improving both physical health and emotional strength. By offering services such as personal training, group classes, and wellness guidance, the organization seeks to help women achieve healthier lifestyles while fostering a sense of belonging.  
Overtime, SFS has positioned itself as more than just a fitness centre. It represents a movement that advocates for women’s empowerment and safety in South Africa. By responding to the social challenges faced by many women, the organization contributes to creating a positive and uplifting space where women can reclaim confidence, build strength, and support one another.  
 
  
 Market Problem:  
 Many women in South Africa face safety concerns, intimidation, and limited access to female-focused fitness environments, which discourages them from participating in regular exercise. This creates a significant gap in the market for a women-only gym like StrongHer that provides a safe, comfortable, and empowering fitness space. Research shows that 79% of South African women feel unsafe when running or exercising in public spaces, often due to harassment or fear of attack. This fear limits their ability to engage in physical activity freely and consistently. Additionally, gender inequality in sports and fitness access remains an issue. In South Africa, only about 23% of women participate in sports, and participation rates are even lower for some groups of women. This highlights a large untapped market of women who could benefit from more accessible and supportive fitness environments.
